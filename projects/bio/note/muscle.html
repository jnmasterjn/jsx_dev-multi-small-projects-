Structure of Skeletal Muscles

Skeletal Muscle (大) -> Bundles (Myofibrils) -> Sarcomeres (小)

Sarcomeres

Sarcomeres -> (a) Actins (b) Myosin

Muscle Contraction

Key Terms: Sliding Filament Theory, Power Stroke, Cross-Bridge

Steps

  1. Stimulated by nerve impulses.
  2. Calcium ions release from sarcoplasmic reticulum to sarcoplasm.
  3. Troponin bounded by calcium ions.
  4. Troponin moves Tropomyosin away from blocking myosin binding sites.
  5. Binding sites exposed, allowing myosin to bind to actin.
  1. ATP is required, it binds to myosin head.
  2. This causes myosin to detach from its binding site on actin.
  3. ATP hydrolysed into ADP and Pi.
  4. Myosin head changes position, moving toward the next binding site on actin.
  5. Myosin attaches to the new binding site, forming a CROSS-BRIDGE.
  6. Myosin head pulls actin filament toward the center by a process called POWER STROKE.
  7. Cycle repeated when ATP binds to myosin head, allowing it to detach from actin.
